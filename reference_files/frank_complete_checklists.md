## Pre-Tour Data Review

*Complete this before every tour. Takes 20 minutes. Saves families from facilities they would have regretted.*

**Facility Name:** _______________________________________________

**Date of Review:** _______________________________________________

### Step 1 — Frank Trust Index (whatsfrank.com)

- [ ] FTI overall score noted
  - *If below 35, stop here and consider alternatives*
- [ ] FTI sub-score weaknesses identified
  - *Note which factors are low: staffing, inspections, penalties*

### Step 2 — CMS Care Compare (medicare.gov/care-compare)

- [ ] Special Focus Facility (SFF) status checked
  - *If SFF or SFF Candidate: hard stop, do not proceed*
- [ ] Abuse icon status checked
  - *Active abuse icon: disqualifying*
- [ ] Overall star rating and three component ratings recorded
- [ ] Health Inspection star rating noted separately
- [ ] Staffing star rating noted separately
- [ ] Quality Measure star rating noted separately

### Step 3 — Staffing Data

- [ ] RN hours per resident per day recorded and compared to state average
- [ ] Total nurse staffing hours per resident per day recorded
- [ ] Weekend staffing ratio noted and compared to weekday
  - *Significant gap = staffing problem*
- [ ] Nurse staff turnover rate recorded

### Step 4 — Penalties and Fines

- [ ] Number of fines in last 3 years recorded
- [ ] Total dollar amount of fines recorded
- [ ] Any active payment suspensions noted

### Step 5 — Inspection Report

- [ ] Most recent standard survey downloaded and read
- [ ] Any F600-series citations (abuse/neglect) noted
  - *F600s: read the full narrative*
- [ ] Any F725-730 citations (staffing violations) noted
  - *Federal minimum staffing floor violations*
- [ ] Any F686-690 citations (pressure injury) noted
- [ ] Recurring deficiencies across multiple surveys identified
  - *Same deficiency 2+ cycles = systemic, not isolated*
- [ ] Plan of correction reviewed for specificity

### Step 6 — ProPublica Cross-Reference

- [ ] Facility looked up at projects.propublica.org/nursing-homes
- [ ] Any additional context or discrepancies noted

### Questions to Bring to the Tour

- [ ] 3-5 specific questions drafted from data findings
  - *Write them now, before you walk in*

## Memory Care Tour

*A locked hallway is not memory care. Use this checklist to find out what is actually behind the label.*

**Facility Name:** _______________________________________________

**Tour Date:** _______________________________________________

**Is this a standalone memory care facility or a unit within a larger facility?** _______________________________________________

### The Environment — Is This Genuinely Specialized?

- [ ] Unit is dedicated and secured — not a general wing with a locked door
- [ ] Layout is circular or allows safe walking without dead ends
- [ ] Exit doors secured or disguised appropriately
- [ ] Visual and sensory cues used intentionally throughout the space
  - *Familiar objects, clear wayfinding, calming colors*
- [ ] Dedicated outdoor space confirmed — secured and accessible
- [ ] Lighting adequate — natural light prioritized

### Staffing — The Most Critical Factor

- [ ] Resident-to-aide ratio on this unit specifically confirmed
  - *Not the facility overall — this unit*
- [ ] Staff assigned consistently to same residents across shifts confirmed
  - *Consistent caregivers = familiarity for dementia residents*
- [ ] Staff dementia-specific training confirmed and recency asked
- [ ] Staffing on late afternoon/evening (sundowning window) confirmed
  - *Does staffing increase during 3–9pm? It should.*
- [ ] Staff turnover rate on this unit specifically asked

### Programming and Daily Life

- [ ] Daily programming observed or described in detail
- [ ] Programming designed for dementia residents — not general activities adapted
- [ ] Music therapy or music programming confirmed
- [ ] Outdoor time incorporated into daily schedule
- [ ] Mealtime support for residents who need assistance observed or described

### Medications — The Antipsychotic Question

- [ ] Antipsychotic medication rate for this unit asked
  - *Compare to state and national averages on CMS Care Compare*
- [ ] Protocol for managing behavioral symptoms without medication explained
  - *Staff response to agitation, wandering, resistance to care*
- [ ] Medical director involvement in behavioral care confirmed

### Communication and Family

- [ ] Family communication protocol explained — who calls, how quickly
- [ ] Care plan meeting process for memory care residents explained
- [ ] What happens when dementia progresses beyond what unit can manage?
  - *Specific answer required. Vague answers are information.*

### Financial

- [ ] Base monthly rate confirmed in writing
- [ ] Memory care premium above standard AL or nursing home confirmed
  - *Write it: $____/month additional*
- [ ] Medicaid memory care beds available — and will resident stay if payer changes?

### Next Steps

- [ ] Unannounced return visit scheduled on a different day and time
- [ ] Staff members spoken with directly — ask how long they have worked on this unit

## Home Care and Home Health Agency

*Home care and home health are not the same thing. One is covered by Medicare. One is not. Use the right checklist for the right service.*

### Home Care Agency — Non-Medical Personal Care

- [ ] Agency employs caregivers directly — not independent contractors
  - *Ask directly. Get the answer in writing.*
- [ ] Background checks confirmed for all caregivers
- [ ] Liability insurance and workers' compensation coverage confirmed
- [ ] Backup coverage policy: who calls when scheduled aide cannot come? How quickly?
- [ ] Supervision model: how often does a supervisor observe caregiver work?
- [ ] Caregiver consistency: will same aide come regularly, or rotate?
  - *Consistency matters especially for dementia and anxiety*
- [ ] After-hours contact: direct number, not voicemail only
- [ ] References from current or recent families offered

### Home Care — Services and Costs

- [ ] Complete list of included and excluded services confirmed in writing
- [ ] Hourly rate and minimum hours per visit confirmed in writing
  - *Write it: $____/hour, minimum ____hrs*
- [ ] Weekend and holiday rate premium confirmed
- [ ] Cancellation policy confirmed
- [ ] Live-in care rate confirmed if applicable *(Write it: $____/day)*

### Home Health Agency — Medicare-Certified Skilled Care

- [ ] Medicare certification status confirmed at medicare.gov/care-compare
- [ ] Quality data reviewed on Home Health Compare — hospitalization rate, mobility improvement
- [ ] Any compliance deficiencies in past 3 years reviewed
- [ ] Physician order confirmed or will be obtained
- [ ] Homebound status criteria met and documented
- [ ] Specific disciplines available: RN, PT, OT, speech therapy
- [ ] Frequency of skilled nursing visits confirmed
- [ ] After-hours on-call coverage: nurse employed by agency, not answering service
- [ ] Aide services included alongside skilled care confirmed
- [ ] When Medicare ends: what ongoing help will my parent still need?
  - *Ask before Medicare ends, not after*

## Medicare Quick Reference

*Print this. Keep it accessible during any hospitalization. These three pages have saved families thousands of dollars.*

### The Observation Status Script — Ask This at Every Hospitalization

> **"Is my parent formally admitted as an inpatient, or are they on observation status?"**

Observation status days do not count toward the 3-day qualifying stay for Medicare SNF coverage. Get the answer in writing. Ask the case manager directly.

- [ ] Asked: is my parent inpatient or on observation status?
- [ ] MOON notice received (required if on observation status)
- [ ] Requested conversion to inpatient status if appropriate
- [ ] Attending physician supports inpatient status in documentation

### The 100-Day SNF Benefit — Day by Day

| Days | Your Cost | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Days 1–20 | $0 out of pocket | Medicare pays 100% |
| Days 21–100 | ~$204/day coinsurance (2026) | Medigap may cover |
| Day 101+ | 100% private pay | Medicare ends |

*The 100-day benefit ends when skilled care ends — not automatically at day 100.*

### When Medicare Ends Early — The NOMNC Appeal

When the facility says Medicare is ending, they must give you a written Notice of Medicare Non-Coverage (NOMNC). You have the right to appeal.

- [ ] NOMNC received and dated
- [ ] Appeal requested by noon of day before coverage scheduled to end
  - *Call your BFCC-QIO at qioprogram.org — find your state's QIO*
- [ ] Parent remains in facility during appeal — do not pack up the room
- [ ] Appeal decision received within 1 business day — free of charge

### Medicare Advantage — Call Before Any SNF Admission

- [ ] Does this SNF require prior authorization from my plan?
- [ ] Is this SNF in-network for my specific plan?
- [ ] What is my daily cost-sharing structure?
- [ ] What is the internal appeal process if coverage is denied?

## Resident Rights Reference

*Every resident of a Medicare or Medicaid certified nursing home has these rights under federal law. They are not suggestions. They are enforceable.*

*Print this page. Keep it in your parent's room. In a visible place.*

### The Ten Rights

- [ ] **The right to be treated with dignity and respect**
  - *Protected from abuse, neglect, humiliation, and exploitation. Regardless of cognitive status.*
- [ ] **The right to participate in their own care**
  - *Informed of medical condition. Involved in care planning. Right to refuse any treatment or medication.*
- [ ] **The right to access their own information**
  - *Medical records within 24 hours of request. Copies within 2 business days.*
- [ ] **The right to privacy and confidentiality**
  - *Staff must knock. Mail cannot be opened without consent. Phone calls are private.*
- [ ] **The right to manage their own finances**
  - *Facility must maintain separate accounts and provide quarterly statements.*
- [ ] **The right to voice grievances without retaliation**
  - *Cannot be discharged, threatened, or penalized for complaining.*
- [ ] **The right to be free from unnecessary restraints**
  - *Physical and chemical restraints require physician order, clinical justification, and consent.*
- [ ] **The right to remain in the facility**
  - *30 days written notice required. Valid discharge reasons are limited. Right to appeal.*
- [ ] **The right to make advance directives**
  - *Facility must honor living will, healthcare proxy, and POLST.*
- [ ] **The right to organize**
  - *Right to form and participate in a Resident Council.*

### If a Right Is Violated

- [ ] Document what happened: date, time, what was observed, who was present
- [ ] Raise it with the Director of Nursing or Administrator: specific, factual, with documentation
- [ ] Contact the Long-Term Care Ombudsman if facility response is inadequate
  - *Eldercare Locator: 1-800-677-1116 · eldercare.acl.gov · Free service*
- [ ] File a complaint with the state survey agency if problem persists
  - *Can file at medicare.gov/care-compare or by calling state agency · Can file anonymously*
- [ ] Call 911 for any situation involving immediate danger to your parent
  - *Safety first. Paperwork after.*

## Financial Planning Guide

*The families who plan early protect more. The ones who discover the rules when the money is running out have fewer options.*

### Immediate Actions — Do These Now

- [ ] Locate all insurance policies: long-term care, life, annuities
- [ ] Identify whether parent is a veteran or surviving spouse of a veteran
  - *Research VA Aid and Attendance eligibility at va.gov/pension/aid-attendance-housebound*
- [ ] Identify whether parent has a long-term care insurance policy
  - *Locate the policy. Verify it is current. Identify benefit triggers and elimination period.*
- [ ] Review advance directives, healthcare proxy, and financial POA — do they exist?

### Before Choosing Any Facility

- [ ] Complete ancillary fee schedule requested in writing
- [ ] Maximum possible monthly cost at highest care level modeled
- [ ] Bed hold policy and daily rate confirmed in writing
- [ ] Medicaid acceptance and Medicaid bed availability confirmed if applicable
- [ ] Payer source change policy confirmed: will parent be allowed to stay?

### If Medicaid May Be Needed Within 5 Years

- [ ] Elder law attorney consultation scheduled
  - *Find one at naela.org. Two hours now protects decades of savings.*
- [ ] Countable vs. exempt assets identified with attorney guidance
- [ ] Community Spouse Resource Allowance (CSRA) calculated for married couples
- [ ] Any asset transfers in past 5 years reviewed for look-back implications
  - *Do not move any assets before this conversation*
- [ ] Medicaid eligibility timeline projected

### Long-Term Care Insurance — If Policy Exists

- [ ] Policy located and current premium payment confirmed
- [ ] Benefit triggers identified: ____ ADLs or cognitive impairment
- [ ] Elimination period confirmed: ____ days
- [ ] Daily or monthly benefit amount confirmed: $____
- [ ] Benefit period confirmed: ____ years or lifetime
- [ ] Inflation protection confirmed: yes / no
- [ ] Covered settings confirmed: home care / AL / nursing home / memory care
- [ ] Claim filed immediately when benefit triggers are met
  - *Do not wait. Elimination period does not start until claim is filed.*

### Veterans Benefits

- [ ] Wartime military service confirmed: conflict era ____
- [ ] Honorable discharge confirmed
- [ ] Aid and Attendance application initiated through a VSO — free of charge
  - *VSOs: American Legion, VFW, DAV · Do not pay a third party to file*

### Family Financial Conversation

- [ ] Full family conversation about financial capacity held honestly
- [ ] Realistic private pay runway calculated: approximately ____ months/years
- [ ] Plan for when private pay resources are exhausted discussed
- [ ] SNF social worker consulted: given this situation, what is the realistic financial trajectory?
